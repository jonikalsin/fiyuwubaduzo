Who is Jeannie Mai Jenkins?

Jeannie Mai, the accomplished talk show host, actress, and celebrity stylist, has made a name for herself through her bold fashion statements and expertise. Her name, which means “God is gracious” in Hebrew, is fitting for someone who has achieved so much.

Jeannie’s career has made her a co-host of the popular daytime talk show “The Real,” where she gained widespread recognition. She has also garnered fame through her work on the fashion show “How Do I Look?” Her achievements are a testament to her hard work and dedication.

But Jeannie’s rise to fame has not been without its challenges. As a makeup artist trainee, she worked hard to develop her skills and eventually became a sought-after stylist, working with some of the biggest names in the industry. Her recent engagement with the famous American rapper Jeezy has only added to her star power, and there seems to be no stopping this unstoppable force in the entertainment industry.

Quick facts

Early life

The twists and turns of life marked Jeannie Mai Jenkins’ childhood. She was born on January 4th, 1979, in San Jose, California, and grew up to be a strong, wise, and independent woman, thanks to the teachings of her parents. Jeannie has two younger brothers, Dennis and Daniel.

Jeannie Mai is a Capricorn who initially practiced Buddhism but later converted to Christianity. Despite facing various challenges, Jeannie’s unrelenting spirit and passion for helping others have made her a beloved public figure in the entertainment industry.

Despite being an American citizen, Jeannie’s heritage is deeply rooted in Asian culture. She is of Vietnamese and Chinese descent and speaks fluent Vietnamese and Spanish and English. Jeannie deeply respects and admires her mother’s birthplace and traditions, as her mother had come to the US by boat and was rescued by the US Navy.

Jeannie’s talents are diverse, from makeup artistry to styling to hosting. She pursued a degree in Communications at De Anza College after attending Milpitas High School in Milpitas, California. Jeannie Mai stands at 1.62 meters tall.

Jeannie’s success in the media has not stopped her from giving back to the community. She has worked with NightLight International, an organization that works towards helping victims of sex trafficking and exploitation. Jeannie’s inspiring story of her struggles in America with her mother has resonated with people, particularly those from Vietnam. Jeannie’s purpose in life is to reach out to those in need and lend a listening ear.

Parents

Olivia Tu Tram Mai

Jeannie Mai’s parents, Olivia Tu Tram Mai and her father, who is Chinese, raised their daughter in San Jose, California, instilling in her a deep appreciation for her Asian roots.

Jeannie’s relationship with her mother has been a roller-coaster ride. Olivia married her second husband, Ted, without informing her children, which surprised Jeannie and her siblings. Eight years later, the truth came to light when Jeannie found out that Uncle Ted was their stepfather.

Olivia and Ted’s relationship started in a chat room for single Vietnamese. After talking online for four months, they met in Houston, where they instantly clicked. They eventually got married, but they kept the news from their children, not knowing how they would react. Jeannie only learned about it eight years later and was happy for her mother.

However, their relationship was not always smooth sailing. Jeannie experienced abuse from a family member from nine until she was sixteen or seventeen. When she told her mother about it, Olivia didn’t believe her, which led to Jeannie running away from home when she was only sixteen. The two didn’t reconcile for eight years until Jeannie was twenty-four.

After their reconciliation, Jeannie explained everything to her mother, who then confronted Jeannie’s abuser.

James Mai

Jeannie Mai’s father, James Mai, was Chinese, and her mother, Olivia, ran away with him at a young age due to her father’s mistreatment of her. Olivia met James in Texas, where he was working as an assembly line manager, and they had Jeannie when they were both young. Despite their situation, they kept her and had two other children, Daniel and Dennis.

The details of Jeannie’s parents’ relationship after their separation are not widely known. Her father is in a long-term relationship with his girlfriend. James Mai appeared on Jeannie’s Youtube series, Hello Hunnay.

Siblings

Jeannie Mai Jenkins has two younger brothers, Daniel and Dennis Mai. The three siblings are close, and Jeannie has talked about her brothers in various interviews and on her social media platforms. Her younger brother, Dennis Mai, is an artist, and Jeannie has often shared his artwork on her Instagram account to support him.

relationships

Jeannie Mai’s first marriage was to Freddy Harteis, whom she met in August 2007 and married soon after. However, Jeannie believes they rushed into marriage, and the union was not sustainable. The couple frequently argued over finances, and neither was happy, leading to their decision to file for divorce in October 2017, which was legally finalized in December 2018.

Jeannie has since moved on and is currently married to American rapper Jeezy. The couple first met on the set of “The Real” and began dating years later, with their first date being a romantic sushi dinner followed by salsa dancing. Jeezy proposed to Jeannie on March 27th, 2020, and they have been happily in love.

Jeannie and Jeezy’s relationship is marked by their mutual passion for life and their positive influence on each other. Their fans can see their cute PDAs on Jeannie’s Instagram page. Jeannie and Jeezy have had failed relationships in the past, with Jeezy being a father to a son from a previous relationship.

During the quarantine period, Jeezy proposed to Jeannie Mai with a stunning 4-5 carat marquise cut diamond ring worth around USD 150,000. Jeezy treated Jeannie to a romantic date night featuring Vietnamese cuisine to make the proposal extra special. They had planned to visit Vietnam together but couldn’t due to the pandemic.

The couple initially planned a wedding in Lake Como, France but had to change their plans due to COVID-19. They opted for an intimate ceremony with their immediate family and close friends in their garden, with strict COVID-19 protocols in place. Jeannie wore a gorgeous wedding gown by Galia Lahav and a 15-foot Galia Lahav veil, while Jeezy wore a custom design by Teofilo Flor.

The wedding ceremony also included a Vietnamese tea ceremony, organized by Jeannie’s brother, to honor their parents by pouring tea as a gesture of welcoming the groom into the bride’s family and vice versa. The guests were requested to wear off-white attire to represent peace, and the wedding had live music performances by Trap Jazz and Tyrese.

Jeezy

Jeezy, whose real name is Jay Wayne Jenkins, is a well-known rapper, singer, and songwriter. He gained fame with his major-label debut, “Let’s Get It: Thug Motivation,” and started his music career under Young Jeezy in 2001. Jeezy has released ten studio albums and numerous hit singles, including “Soul Survivor,” “I Luv It,” “Go Getta,” and “Put On.”

Despite his successful career, Jeezy faced several legal issues, including arrests for firearm possession, DUI, and threats after an alleged fight with his son in 2014. However, he has not faced any charges since he began dating Jeannie Mai.

Jeannie has posted several adorable photos and videos of Monaco from backstage, including one video of the baby admiring her glittering dress.

career

Jeannie Mai’s career began as a trainee make-up artist at Mac Cosmetics when she was just eighteen. She gained experience working with celebrities like Alicia Keys, and her skills led to her being hired as a make-up stylist for MTV’s Total Request Live and Good Day Sacramento.

She auditioned for local television networks with self-written scripts in 2003, eventually hiring her as co-host of the Asian-American magazine-style show, Stir on an international channel. She then hosted her music countdown show on the California Music Channel and became an entertainment reporter for the WB’s “The Daily Mix” in her mid-20s.

Jeannie Mai has hosted numerous television shows and beauty pageants throughout her career. She significantly impacted women through her expertise as a makeover expert on “How Do I Look?” She is most popularly known as the co-host of the Emmy Award-winning daytime talk show, “The Real,” where she has been a co-host since 2013 alongside Adrienne Bailon.

In addition to her hosting career, Jeannie Mai is also a renowned celebrity stylist, and she has a YouTube channel called Hello Hunnay with Jeannie Mai.

Jeannie Mai has showcased her hosting talents throughout her career, and people love her stage presence. She has worked hard to make a name for herself in the entertainment industry and is now a successful celebrity stylist and talk show host.
